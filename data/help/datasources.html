Data Sources

To realistically depict the Solar System, Cosmographia must integrate astronomical data from many different sources.

Planet maps
These are nearly always mosaics of images acquired by spacecraft missions. No synthetic surface maps have been used; unmapped areas are shown in a uniform color, chosen to match the average color of the surface while still indicating that no data is available. The full list of texture sources is available here.

Shape models
3D models for non-spherical moons and asteroids. The geometry is derived from real sources where available. Where no shape model exists for a body, a model of a similarly sized body may be substituted. These substitutions are noted in the description text.

Ephemeris data
Orbital ephemerides for planets and moons and trajectories for spacecraft. JPL's DE406 ephemeris is used for the positions of the planets, Pluto and the Moon. Semi-analytic theories are employed to compute the positions of the major natural satellites of the Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Mars. To reproduce some of the Cassini encounters with moons of Saturn, it has been necessary to supplement these theories with tables of Chebyshev polynomials covering the time period of the mission.

Star catalog
Cosmographia is using the all stars from the Tycho-2 catalog that are visually brighter than magnitude 10, or approximately 950,000 stars. The Milky Way map was generated from the NOMAD star catalog by summing the contributions of about half a billion stars of magnitude 11 or less.
