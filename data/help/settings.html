Graphics Settings
Milky Way Show the Milky Galaxy in the background
Clouds Display cloud layers on planets that have them (such as Earth and Venus)
Atmospheres Enable atmospheric scattering effect; makes Earth look much more realistic
Eclipse Shadows Show shadows cast by planets and moons
Other Shadows Show shadows cast by small, irregular bodies (spacecraft, asteroids, etc.)
Reflections Show reflections on polished and metallic surfaces. This effect is visible only for a few spacecraft
Sun Glare Show a glare halo around the Sun when it isn't obscured
Anaglyph Stereo Enable this to get a stereo 3D effect when viewing with red/blue glasses
Star Brightness Adjusts the apparent brightness of the stars. The default setting approximates what you'd see with your eye on a perfectly dark night. Decrease the brightness to simulate light pollution, or increase it to show stars as they'd appear in a long-exposure photograph.
Extra Light Adjusts the amount of artificial light added to shadowed areas. For realism, the extra light should be disabled by moving the slider all the way to the left. But for ease of navigation, it may be better to add a bit of light to the scene.
Anti-aliasing When enabled, anti-aliasing greatly improves visual quality by smoothing jagged edges. Anti-aliasing may make Cosmographia run less smoothly on slower computers. On newer, faster computers, it's best to leave anti-aliasing enabled all the time. Cosmographia must be restarted for changes in the anti-aliasing setting to take effect.
